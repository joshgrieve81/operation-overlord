Interfaces that behave like places.

A lofi spatial portfolio prototype: case-study frames orbit the visitor, react to scroll velocity, and form a navigable field of work.

Enter the field Scroll slowly — the archive rearranges itself.

01 / Spatial archive

Work samples float around the viewer instead of sitting in a grid.

Each grey plane is a placeholder for a product screen, launch still, research artifact, or motion study. As final imagery arrives, the planes can become textured cards with shader treatments and case-study routing.

02 / Design posture

Research as atmosphere. Prototypes as evidence. Motion as wayfinding.

  • Scroll changes camera depth, orbit radius, and editorial tone.
  • Hover/drag intent can later “pull” a project into focus.
  • Reduced motion users get a calmer, legible experience.

03 / Case-study choreography

Instead of revealing cards, the user passes through constellations.

The next iteration should map each cluster to a designer strength: product strategy, UX systems, visual craft, experiments, and shipped outcomes.

04 / Closing

Make the portfolio feel discovered, not browsed.

Prototype v0.1. Grey boxes today, image-driven cinematic portfolio tomorrow.
